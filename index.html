About

I am an Assistant Professor in the University of Toronto's Department of Mathematics. I completed my Ph.D. in Spring 2020 at the University of Chicago, under the supervision of Matt Emerton. Here is a link to my CV.

I work in number theory and representation theory. These days, I think about asymptotic and statistical aspects of automorphic representations, and the cohomology of arithmetic groups. You can watch a talk I gave about my work for a general mathematical audience.

I am from Québec and I have a French first name. For anglophones, it is pronounced "Matild" and rhymes with "build" and "thrilled".
